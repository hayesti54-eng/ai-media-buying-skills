# Meta Ad Copy Generator

**Platform:** Meta Ads
**Category:** creative
**Tier:** free

## Purpose

Generate platform-native ad copy for Meta Ads placements — including primary text, headline, and description — that is structured to stop the scroll, deliver the offer clearly, and drive click intent within the character constraints and cognitive patterns of Facebook and Instagram feeds, Stories, and Reels. Copy is written to work in conjunction with the visual creative, not to compensate for it.

## When to Use

- When launching a new campaign and need copy variations for A/B testing
- When existing copy is fatigued and a creative refresh is needed
- When testing different messaging angles (pain-point, benefit-led, social proof, urgency, curiosity)
- When adapting copy across audience segments (cold prospecting vs. warm retargeting vs. past purchasers)
- When copy needs to be adapted for different placements: Feed (longer primary text acceptable) vs. Stories/Reels (copy must work in 3 seconds or less on the visual)
- When writing copy for a new offer, promotion, or product launch
- When auditing existing copy against Meta's ad policy to identify potential disapproval triggers

## Inputs Required

- Product or service name and core offer
- Target audience: demographics, psychographics, pain points, desires
- Campaign objective: awareness, traffic, leads, purchases, app installs
- Offer mechanics: price point, discount, free trial, limited time, guarantee, bonus
- Key differentiator vs. competitors
- Tone of brand: professional, casual, bold, empathetic, direct
- Placement(s) targeted: Feed, Stories, Reels, Marketplace, Right Column
- Audience temperature: cold (no prior exposure), warm (visited site / engaged with page), hot (cart abandoner / past customer)
- Any copy restrictions: prohibited claims, regulatory constraints, brand voice guidelines

## What This Skill Does

This skill generates structured ad copy sets — each with a primary text variant, headline variant, and optional description — optimized for the specified placement, audience temperature, and messaging angle. Each copy set follows Meta's character limits, is written to front-load the hook within the first 125 characters of primary text (the visible limit before "See More"), and is designed to complement visual creative rather than duplicate it.

## Analysis Workflow

1. Identify the single most compelling hook for the target audience based on the offer and pain point. The hook is the first sentence of primary text and must do one of three things: interrupt pattern (bold claim), create curiosity (question or gap), or trigger relevance (call out the exact audience).
2. Write the primary text in the following structure: Hook (1-2 sentences, max 125 characters before "See More" truncation) → Value proposition (1-2 sentences: what you offer and why it matters) → Social proof or credibility signal (optional, 1 sentence) → Call to action (direct, specific, action-verb-led).
3. Generate the headline (max 40 characters for most placements) as a direct statement of the offer or outcome. The headline appears below the image/video and is often the last thing read before clicking. It should be complementary to, not repetitive of, the primary text hook.
4. Write the description (max 30 characters) as a supporting reinforcement: price, guarantee, deadline, or secondary benefit.
5. Create three copy variants for each campaign: (a) Pain-point angle — leads with the problem the audience has; (b) Benefit/outcome angle — leads with the transformation or result; (c) Offer/urgency angle — leads directly with the deal or deadline.
6. For retargeting audiences (warm/hot), adjust copy to acknowledge prior exposure: reference the product they viewed, address objections, or create urgency around a specific incentive. Do not use generic awareness copy for warm audiences.
7. For Stories and Reels placements, write a version of the primary text that works as on-screen text within 3-5 words per card or scene. Long primary text is not read in Stories — the visual and the first 5 words of on-screen copy must carry full conversion intent.
8. Check copy against Meta's Advertising Policies: flag any claims involving health outcomes, financial guarantees, prohibited categories (employment, credit, housing), and overly personal attribute references ("Are you struggling with debt?").
9. Deliver copy in a structured format ready for upload into Meta Ads Manager.

## Output Requirements

- Three complete copy sets per request: Pain-point angle, Benefit angle, Offer/urgency angle
- Each set includes: Primary text (full and truncated-at-125-char preview), Headline, Description
- Stories/Reels text overlay version for each set (condensed)
- Policy flag note: any language that risks ad disapproval
- Recommended split test pairing: which two variants to test first and what metric to use as the winner signal

## Platform-Specific Best Practices

- The first 125 characters of primary text are visible without clicking "See More." Every important message must be front-loaded in this window.
- Emojis used strategically in primary text increase Thumbstop on mobile feeds — but use maximum 1-2 per copy block. Emoji-heavy copy looks like spam.
- Questions as hooks outperform statement hooks when they precisely name the audience's exact situation (not vague questions like "Want to save money?").
- CTAs on Meta perform best as specific verbs: "Start your free trial," "Claim your discount," "Book your free consultation" — not "Learn more" or "Click here."
- For Advantage+ Creative, write primary text that can stand alone independent of headline because Meta may serve the primary text in different positions depending on format.

## Guardrails

- Do not write copy that implies personal attributes without permission-based context (e.g., "We know you have diabetes" violates Meta policy).
- Do not make superlative claims ("best," "cheapest," "#1") without a legitimate factual basis.
- Do not write copy designed to exploit emotional vulnerabilities in a manipulative way.
- Avoid ALL CAPS beyond a single word for emphasis. Full caps copy triggers ad review flags.
