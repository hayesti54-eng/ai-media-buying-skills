# Native-Style Ad Reviewer

**Platform:** TikTok Ads
**Category:** creative
**Tier:** pro

## Purpose
Evaluate TikTok ad creatives against native-feel quality standards — the degree to which an ad looks, sounds, and behaves like organic FYP content rather than a paid advertisement — and produce a scored review with specific production and messaging edits that increase native authenticity, improve thumbstop rate, and reduce the unconscious "ad detection" response that causes viewers to swipe.

## When to Use
- Reviewing a finished creative before launch to catch native-feel failures before budget is committed
- Auditing a low-performing creative where hook rate and CTR are both below benchmark
- Evaluating creatives produced by a brand team or traditional video production company before TikTok deployment
- Comparing multiple creative variations to select the most native-feeling option for a cold traffic test
- Onboarding a new creator or production partner and setting TikTok creative quality standards

## Inputs Required
- Creative video file (describe fully if unable to share file) OR detailed description of the creative: visual setup, creator type, opening scene, hook delivery, pacing, text overlays, music, CTA delivery, production quality indicators
- Creative format: UGC talking head, branded polished video, product demo, influencer Spark, animation/motion graphic, text-overlay voiceover
- Whether the creative is intended as Spark Ad (from organic post) or non-Spark (dark post)
- Target audience and campaign objective
- Optional: current performance metrics if the creative is already live (hook rate, CTR, CPA)

## What This Skill Does
This skill scores the creative across eight native-feel dimensions on a 1–5 scale, identifies the specific production and creative elements triggering the viewer's "ad detection" response, and produces a ranked edit list with exact changes needed to increase native authenticity. Higher native scores correlate directly with improved thumbstop rate, lower CPMs, and better algorithm preference on TikTok.

## Analysis Workflow
1. Score Dimension 1 — Visual Authenticity (1–5): Does the creative look like something a real person would film and post on TikTok? Signs of ad feel: studio-quality lighting with obvious softboxes, multiple camera angles cut together, drone shots, graphic lower thirds, brand watermarks in frame. Native signals: handheld or tripod at eye level, natural/ring light, single camera angle, real environment.
2. Score Dimension 2 — Hook Energy and Delivery (1–5): Does the hook feel spontaneous and genuine, or rehearsed and performed? Native delivery is direct eye contact, slightly imperfect cadence, genuine emotion or emphasis. Ad delivery is smooth, perfect, over-enunciated, and reads as scripted.
3. Score Dimension 3 — Pacing and Edit Style (1–5): Native TikTok pacing uses jump cuts, quick scene changes, and a conversational rhythm that mirrors organic creator content. Slow transitions, cinematic fades, graphic wipes, and deliberate product showcase pacing all signal "advertisement" to the viewer's subconscious.
4. Score Dimension 4 — Text Overlay and Caption Style (1–5): Native caption style uses bold subtitles with emoji, platform-standard fonts, and conversational text fragments ("Wait for it," "Not sponsored," "Fr though"). Corporate sans-serif, logo-branded overlay systems, and formal subtitle formatting read as branded ad content.
5. Score Dimension 5 — Audio Landscape (1–5): Native TikTok audio is creator voice dominant (not mixed under music), uses trending TikTok-licensed tracks at low volume as ambiance, or follows creator-voice-only norms. High-production music beds, voice-over polish, and silence where organic noise would naturally exist all signal ad production.
6. Score Dimension 6 — Brand Presence Timing (1–5): Where in the video does branding, logo, or product label first appear? Appearing in the first 3 seconds is a critical native-feel failure for cold traffic. Native-feel ads reveal the brand at the offer reveal stage (8–12 seconds), not the hook stage.
7. Score Dimension 7 — CTA Delivery Style (1–5): Native CTAs are casual and integrated: "I'll link it below," "Check the link if you're curious," "Honestly just try it." Ad CTAs are declarative, formal, and feel transactional: "CLICK NOW TO BUY," "Visit our website for more information."
8. Score Dimension 8 — Creator or Talent Fit (1–5): Does the person on screen match the target audience's peer group, aspirational figure, or trusted source of information? Mismatched creator demographics or a visible "actor/model" presentation undermines authenticity.
9. Calculate composite Native-Feel Score (average of 8 dimensions × 12.5 = score out of 100). Classify: 80–100 = Native-Grade, 60–79 = Borderline, 40–59 = Branded Feel, Below 40 = Traditional Ad.
10. Rank edit recommendations by native-feel impact score and produce specific, actionable edits for each failing dimension.

## Output Requirements
- Eight-dimension native-feel scorecard with scores (1–5) and specific failing elements per dimension
- Composite Native-Feel Score (out of 100) with classification tier
- Ranked edit recommendation list: edit title, dimension affected, specific change required, estimated impact on native-feel score
- "Launch as-is" vs. "Edit before launch" vs. "Rebuild" recommendation with rationale
- If Edit: which 3 edits have the highest impact and are most feasible without a full reshoot?
- If Rebuild: what format and structural approach would produce a genuinely native-feel version of this concept?

## Platform-Specific Best Practices
- The single highest-impact improvement for low native-feel creatives is almost always the first 3 seconds. A native open — creator talking directly to camera in a real environment with a genuine statement — can salvage an otherwise branded creative.
- Spark Ads receive a native-feel bonus by default because they include organic post engagement indicators (comment count, like count, share icon) that signal to the viewer that a real person posted this content.
- TikTok users have developed extremely sensitive "ad detection" instincts — research suggests the average TikTok user can identify paid content within 1.5–2 seconds. Every extra native-feel signal buys dwell time.
- Imperfection is a feature, not a flaw. A slightly off-center framing, a natural background noise, a creator who stumbles slightly mid-sentence — these are authenticity signals that improve native-feel scores.
- Sound design matters: background sound (kitchen ambient, outdoor noise, TV playing quietly) in a UGC creative significantly increases the authenticity score because it signals a real filming environment.

## Guardrails
- Do not score native-feel in isolation from campaign objective — a branded Spark Ad from a verified creator with 500k followers is appropriately branded and should be scored differently than a cold traffic UGC spot.
- Do not recommend removing all brand presence — the goal is delayed and integrated brand introduction, not brand-free content.
- Do not equate low production quality with high native-feel automatically — poorly lit, inaudible, or visually confusing content is not "more native," it is just low quality.
- Never recommend a full rebuild based on native-feel score alone if current performance metrics are above target.

## Example Requests
1. "Review this TikTok ad creative and score it on native feel. It's a 30-second product demo with professional lighting, brand logo intro, and a scripted presenter. Tell me everything that's killing the native feel and exactly how to fix it."
2. "I have two versions of the same ad — one polished brand video and one UGC talking head. Score both on native feel and tell me which to launch first for cold traffic testing."
3. "My creative team keeps producing TikTok ads that look like TV commercials. Give me a scored review of this spot and a rubric I can give them to self-check before submitting."
