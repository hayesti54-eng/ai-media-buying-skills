# Offer-Message Fit Auditor

**Platform:** TikTok Ads
**Category:** creative
**Tier:** pro

## Purpose
Audit the alignment between the advertised offer (the actual product, price, terms, and value proposition) and the creative message being communicated in TikTok ad scripts, briefs, or live creatives. Identify gaps where the message overpromises, underpromises, misrepresents the offer, or fails to communicate the highest-converting value exchange for the target audience — before those gaps waste budget or damage trust.

## When to Use
- A creative is generating clicks and traffic but converting at a low rate, and the landing page has been ruled out as the cause
- Scaling a new offer to TikTok and want to validate that the message actually communicates the offer's true value before committing creative budget
- Multiple creative angles are being tested and you want to pre-score which message best matches the offer's most compelling value
- Performance was strong and has declined despite stable creative and audience — offer fatigue or message commoditization may be the cause
- Reviewing a creative produced by a copywriter or creative team who may not have deep product knowledge

## Inputs Required
- Full offer details: product/service name, price, what the customer receives, key benefits, any guarantees, refund/return terms, delivery timeframe, and what makes it different from alternatives
- Current ad creative script or description (hook, body, CTA)
- Target audience: who is this for and what do they most want? What is their primary objection?
- Awareness stage: cold traffic (Problem Aware), warm traffic (Solution Aware), or hot traffic (Product Aware/retargeting)
- Conversion goal: purchase, lead, app install, trial, booking, sign-up
- Optional: current CVR or post-click metrics if the creative is live

## What This Skill Does
This skill runs a six-layer offer-message fit audit, scoring alignment between what the offer delivers and what the creative promises, and identifies the specific messaging failures that reduce conversion intent. It also identifies untapped value angles within the offer that are more compelling to the target audience than what the current message communicates.

## Analysis Workflow
1. Deconstruct the offer into its core value components:
   - Primary Outcome (what does the customer get or achieve?)
   - Speed Signal (how fast?)
   - Risk Removal (guarantee, free trial, no commitment?)
   - Differentiation (what makes this offer different or better than alternatives the audience is already aware of?)
   - Social Proof Anchor (why should they believe this works?)
2. Audit Layer 1 — Primary Outcome Clarity: Does the creative communicate the primary outcome in the hook or within the first 5 seconds? A TikTok cold audience will not invest time in an ad unless the payoff is visible immediately. Score: Clear / Implied / Missing.
3. Audit Layer 2 — Promise-to-Delivery Accuracy: Does the creative make claims that the offer cannot fulfill? Overpromising destroys trust post-click and inflates CVR without increasing true sales. Flag any language that exaggerates speed, results, or certainty beyond what the offer genuinely delivers.
4. Audit Layer 3 — Risk-Removal Communication: Does the creative mention the guarantee, free trial, or low-commitment entry point? This is the single highest-converting message element for cold TikTok traffic and is frequently omitted. If the offer has a strong risk-removal component and the creative does not mention it, flag as a high-impact gap.
5. Audit Layer 4 — Differentiation Specificity: Does the creative communicate why this offer is different? Vague differentiation ("the best solution for X") performs significantly worse than specific differentiation ("unlike X which does Y, this does Z instead"). Score specificity.
6. Audit Layer 5 — Audience Value Hierarchy Match: Does the creative lead with what this specific audience cares about most, or what the brand thinks is most impressive? Audiences care about outcomes and pain relief first, features and mechanisms second. Audit whether the message hierarchy matches the audience's value hierarchy.
7. Audit Layer 6 — CTA Commitment Alignment: Does the CTA ask the audience to take a step that matches the trust level a cold TikTok viewer has for this offer? Asking cold traffic to "Buy Now" on a $500 product without establishing any trust is a CTA commitment gap. The CTA should match the awareness stage.
8. Identify the 3 highest-value untapped message angles within the offer: what is the most compelling benefit, guarantee, or differentiator that the current creative is not using?
9. Produce a message rewrite recommendation for the creative's hook, key benefit statement, and CTA based on the audit findings.

## Output Requirements
- Six-layer offer-message fit scorecard with rating (Clear/Implied/Missing or 1–5) per layer
- Top 3 message gaps ranked by conversion impact
- Top 3 untapped offer value angles not currently in the creative
- Specific rewrite recommendations: suggested hook revision, body benefit statement revision, CTA revision
- Offer-message fit summary classification: Strong Fit / Partial Fit / Weak Fit / Misaligned
- If Misaligned: is the problem the offer, the message, or both? Clear diagnosis required.

## Platform-Specific Best Practices
- TikTok cold traffic has the shortest patience for ambiguous value propositions of any paid platform. The offer's payoff must be implied or stated within the first 3 seconds or the viewer will not grant the creative the time to build the case.
- Risk-removal messaging (guarantees, free trials, money-back) is dramatically underutilized in TikTok advertising. It is the highest-conversion message lever for cold traffic and should almost always appear in the body of conversion-focused creatives.
- Specific numbers outperform vague superlatives in every funnel stage. "Lost 12 pounds in 6 weeks" outperforms "Transform your body" by a significant margin — both in thumbstop and conversion.
- TikTok audiences are highly skeptical of performance claims. Social proof (number of customers, reviews, results data) placed in the body of the creative significantly increases message credibility and CVR.
- For DTC e-commerce offers, the price must appear in the creative (or be implied by the CTA) for conversion-optimized cold traffic campaigns. Hidden prices create post-click friction and landing page abandonment.

## Guardrails
- Do not recommend strengthening offer claims beyond what the product can genuinely deliver — compliance and consumer trust are not negotiable.
- Do not change the offer to fix a message problem — strengthen the communication of what the offer already provides.
- Do not score offer-message fit without understanding the audience's awareness stage — a message that is misaligned for cold traffic may be perfectly appropriate for warm retargeting.
- Never recommend removing risk-removal language from a creative in the name of brevity — if the offer has a guarantee, that guarantee is almost always worth its time in the script.

## Example Requests
1. "Here's my offer: 7-day free trial of a $29/month meal planning app. Here's my current TikTok script. Audit whether my message actually communicates the best version of this offer and tell me what I'm missing."
2. "My TikTok click rate is great but post-click conversion is terrible. I've ruled out the landing page. Audit whether my ad creative is making promises the offer can't keep or failing to communicate what makes this worth buying."
3. "I'm testing 3 message angles for the same offer. Score each one on offer-message fit and rank them by which is most likely to convert cold TikTok traffic."
