# UGC Sourcing Workflow Planner

**Platform:** TikTok Ads
**Category:** creative
**Tier:** agency

## Purpose
Build a repeatable, systematized UGC (User-Generated Content) creator sourcing and production workflow for TikTok Ads — from creator identification and briefing through delivery, review, and ad deployment. This skill is designed for agencies and operators running high-volume TikTok creative pipelines who need consistent UGC output without creative quality degradation or production bottlenecks.

## When to Use
- Launching a new TikTok Ads account and building the initial creative library from scratch
- Current creative pipeline is relying on polished branded video that is underperforming against native-feel content
- Scaling spend requires higher creative velocity (5–10+ new UGC assets per week)
- You are managing multiple client accounts and need a replicable UGC sourcing system
- A top-performing UGC creative is fatiguing and you need a systematic process for rapid iteration and replacement

## Inputs Required
- Brand name, product/service category, and primary offer
- Target audience: age range, gender, key interests, pain points, desired outcomes
- Product price point and purchase consideration length (impulse, moderate, high-consideration)
- Creative performance data if available: which hooks, formats, or creator styles have worked historically
- Budget per UGC asset (fully guided creator: $150–$500; raw footage only: $50–$150; performance-based: negotiated)
- Required deliverables: raw footage, fully edited, multiple hooks per creator, aspect ratios needed (9:16 primary)
- Brand safety requirements and content restrictions
- Timeline: when are creatives needed in TikTok Ads Manager?

## What This Skill Does
This skill builds a complete UGC sourcing and production workflow document covering: creator persona requirements, sourcing platform selection, outreach templates, creative brief structure, review/approval protocol, editing direction, and ad deployment checklist. It outputs a repeatable SOP that a media buyer, creative strategist, or account manager can execute consistently.

## Analysis Workflow
1. Define the creator persona. Based on target audience inputs, specify the ideal UGC creator profile: age range, gender, lifestyle indicators, communication style (energetic vs. authoritative vs. relatable peer), camera comfort level, and content aesthetic (raw/authentic vs. polished casual). This is not about follower count — UGC creators need zero following.
2. Identify sourcing platforms: TikTok Creator Marketplace (for Spark Ad permission + creator sourcing), Billo, Fiverr (verified TikTok UGC category), Insense, Minea Creator, direct DMs to micro-creators (10k–100k TikTok following), or existing customer pool for authentic testimonial UGC.
3. Write the creator outreach sequence: initial DM/email (3–4 sentences max, lead with product value, end with clear ask), follow-up message (sent at 48 hours if no reply), onboarding message post-acceptance (brief overview before sending full brief).
4. Build the creative brief structure for the UGC creator:
   - Product explanation (2–3 sentences, plain language, no jargon)
   - Desired outcome / transformation the product delivers
   - Hook options: provide 3–5 hook starters and let the creator adapt to their natural voice
   - Required content elements: product on-screen, specific talking point, CTA phrase
   - Prohibited elements: competitor mentions, specific claims, music that creates copyright issues
   - Tone direction: conversational, not scripted. "Talk to the camera like you're texting a friend about this."
   - Technical requirements: 9:16 aspect ratio, good lighting (natural or ring light), clear audio, minimum 15 seconds
5. Define deliverables clearly: raw footage submission requirements, number of hook variations (minimum 2 per creator), whether creator edits or raw only, caption overlay requirements, revision rounds allowed.
6. Build the review and approval checklist: brand safety review, compliance check, quality threshold check (audio, lighting, hook delivery), Spark Ads authorization request if applicable.
7. Define the editing brief for each raw UGC batch: caption style, text overlay placement, music (TikTok-licensed commercial tracks), pacing notes, hook cut selection from raw footage, CTA screen if needed.
8. Output the full SOP as a workflow with roles assigned (creative strategist, editor, media buyer, client approval if agency).

## Output Requirements
- Creator persona specification document
- Sourcing platform recommendation with rationale by budget tier
- Outreach message templates (initial, follow-up, onboarding)
- Complete UGC creative brief template (brandable, reusable)
- Production deliverables checklist with file naming convention
- Review and approval workflow with turnaround time benchmarks
- Editing brief template for raw UGC footage
- Ad deployment checklist: naming convention, Spark Auth code collection, UTM tagging, pixel event assignment
- Cost-per-asset benchmarks by creator tier and deliverable scope

## Platform-Specific Best Practices
- TikTok's algorithm favors native-feel content. UGC filmed on an iPhone without visible production equipment consistently outperforms studio-lit branded content for cold traffic acquisition.
- Spark Ads authorization from the creator allows their organic post to be boosted as a paid ad — this is the highest-trust ad format on TikTok because it retains social proof (comments, likes, shares) from the organic post.
- Creator voice authenticity is the most important quality variable in TikTok UGC. Fully scripted, word-for-word delivery reads as inauthentic. Provide bullet points and let creators adapt — the imperfection is the performance signal.
- Brief for multiple hooks per creator. A single creator can deliver 3–5 different hooks in one filming session at minimal incremental cost. This dramatically reduces cost per test and accelerates iteration cadence.
- Iteration cadence target for growth-stage TikTok accounts: minimum 3 new UGC creatives entering rotation per week. High-scale accounts run 7–10 new creative tests per week.

## Guardrails
- Do not use creators with large followings as a substitute for quality UGC — follower count is irrelevant for paid ad performance and inflates cost unnecessarily.
- Do not require creators to follow a word-for-word script — it destroys the native-feel advantage that makes UGC work on TikTok.
- Do not launch UGC creatives without completing the brand safety review — TikTok's ad policy flags can silently suppress delivery on non-compliant creatives.
- Never skip the Spark Ad authorization request when the creator has an organic post — the social proof uplift is too valuable to forfeit.
