# Spark Ads Strategy Planner

**Platform:** TikTok Ads
**Category:** launch
**Tier:** pro

## Purpose
Build a complete Spark Ads strategy for a TikTok Ads account — covering creator selection, post selection criteria, authorization workflow, boosting configuration, and performance monitoring. Spark Ads are TikTok's highest-trust ad format because they boost real organic posts with existing social proof, and a properly executed Spark Ads strategy consistently outperforms non-Spark (dark post) creatives on thumbstop rate, engagement rate, and often CPA.

## When to Use
- Launching TikTok ads for the first time and want to start with the highest-converting creative format
- Current non-Spark creatives are underperforming and native-feel is suspected as the cause
- Building a creator partnership program that doubles as a paid media creative pipeline
- Running a Spark Ads test alongside non-Spark dark posts to validate the format advantage for your specific offer
- Scaling an existing creator content program into a paid amplification strategy

## Inputs Required
- Brand name, product/service, and campaign objective
- Creator relationship status: brand's own TikTok account, existing creator partnerships, creators sourced via TikTok Creator Marketplace, or new creators to be identified
- Existing organic TikTok content available for boosting (describe if available: engagement levels, post topic, creator type)
- Campaign budget allocated to Spark Ads
- Target audience and awareness stage
- Whether the goal is to boost existing high-performing organic posts or create content specifically for Spark Ads boosting
- CPA or ROAS target

## What This Skill Does
This skill builds a complete Spark Ads execution strategy: creator and post selection criteria, TikTok Creator Marketplace sourcing guidance, authorization workflow, boosting configuration recommendations (bid strategy, audience, creative formatting), and performance benchmarks. It also produces a side-by-side comparison framework for Spark vs. non-Spark testing if applicable.

## Analysis Workflow
1. Define the Spark Ads sourcing strategy. Choose from three models:
   - Model A — Boost Own Content: boost the brand's own TikTok organic posts. Best for brands with an established, high-engagement TikTok presence.
   - Model B — Creator Partnership Amplification: partner with UGC creators or influencers to produce content designed for organic posting, then boost top performers as Spark Ads.
   - Model C — Creator Marketplace Discovery: identify creators in the TikTok Creator Marketplace who have existing posts about the product category or who can be paid to create and post content.
   Recommend the appropriate model based on inputs.
2. Define creator selection criteria for Model B and C:
   - Content style: does the creator's organic content aesthetic match the target audience's FYP? Prioritize creators whose organic content style mirrors the target audience's consumption preferences.
   - Engagement rate: target 3–8% engagement rate on recent posts (likes + comments + shares / views). Follower count is secondary.
   - Audience authenticity: check for bot-inflated engagement (unrealistic comment patterns, sudden follower spikes).
   - Brand alignment: creator's content persona and values align with the product category.
   - Creator willingness to authorize: confirm creator is willing to grant Spark Ad authorization before production investment.
3. Define post selection criteria for boosting:
   - Organic engagement signals: posts with existing likes, comments, and shares carry social proof into the paid promotion, increasing trust signals.
   - Hook performance: review the post's organic retention — does it have a strong hook? (Check TikTok Analytics for the post's average watch time and completion rate.)
   - Offer clarity: does the post communicate the product benefit clearly enough for paid distribution to a cold audience?
   - Policy compliance: verify the post does not contain claims, music, or visual elements that would violate TikTok's paid promotion policies.
4. Walk through the Spark Ad authorization workflow:
   - Via TikTok Creator Marketplace: creator grants authorization directly within the marketplace. Authorization valid for 30 days.
   - Via Direct Authorization Code: creator goes to TikTok Ads > Creative > Authorization and generates a code. Buyer enters code in the Spark Ad creative setup. Code expires in 30 days — build a renewal reminder into the workflow.
   - Brand's own account posts: brand can directly authorize from its own TikTok account via TikTok Ads Manager.
5. Configure the Spark Ad in TikTok Ads Manager:
   - Select the authorized post as the creative asset
   - Set CTA button appropriate to campaign objective
   - Confirm destination URL is appended with UTM parameters
   - Audience targeting: Spark Ads perform well on Broad Targeting due to the native quality signal, but can also run against Interest stacks
6. Set performance benchmarks: Spark Ads should achieve 20–35% higher hook rate than non-Spark equivalents, lower CPMs (5–15% lower on average due to algorithm preference for high-quality native content), and 15–25% better CTR from the native-feel and social proof elements.
7. Design Spark vs. non-Spark split test if requested: same offer, same hook direction, same creative format — one version as a boosted organic Spark post, one version as a dark non-Spark post. Run for 7 days minimum.

## Output Requirements
- Spark Ads sourcing model recommendation with rationale
- Creator selection scorecard criteria
- Post selection checklist (7-point pre-boost verification)
- Step-by-step Spark Ad authorization workflow (with authorization expiry management protocol)
- Campaign configuration recommendations: objective, bid strategy, audience type, CTA
- Performance benchmarks to validate Spark Ads advantage in account: expected hook rate, CPM, and CTR lift vs. non-Spark baseline
- Spark vs. non-Spark test structure if applicable
- Authorization renewal calendar template for managing creator authorizations at scale

## Platform-Specific Best Practices
- Spark Ads retain all organic engagement from the original post. A post with 500 likes and 100 comments before boosting carries those trust signals into the paid delivery — this social proof effect compounds as the Spark Ad accumulates additional paid engagement.
- TikTok's algorithm rewards Spark Ads with preferential auction treatment because they distribute existing, quality-verified content rather than dark post creatives. This results in measurably lower CPMs for equivalent creative quality.
- Creator Marketplace Spark Ads from creators with 10k–100k followers consistently outperform mega-influencer posts for conversion objectives — the micro-creator's relatability and engagement rate drives higher trust signals for cold traffic.
- The strongest Spark Ads are posts that already have organic traction — even 50–200 organic views with a 5%+ engagement rate signals quality to TikTok's algorithm before any paid budget is committed.
- Spark Ads can run in combination with the same creator's content used as a non-Spark dark post in a different ad set — this allows full-funnel creative coverage without additional production cost.

## Guardrails
- Do not boost an organic post as a Spark Ad without verifying the post contains no licensed music (commercial music rights required for paid promotion — TikTok's commercial music library must be used).
- Do not run Spark Ads without confirming the authorization code is valid and not expired — expired authorizations result in ad rejection or silent delivery failure.
- Do not select creators for Spark Ads based on follower count alone — engagement rate and content-audience fit are the performance predictors.
- Never boost a post without reviewing it for TikTok ad policy compliance — organic posts can contain claims or content that passes organic review but fails paid promotion review.
