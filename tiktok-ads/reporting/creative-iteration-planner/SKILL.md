# Creative Iteration Planner

**Platform:** TikTok Ads
**Category:** reporting
**Tier:** pro

## Purpose
Build a systematic, data-driven creative iteration plan for TikTok Ads — taking current creative performance data and translating it into a structured next-round testing roadmap that extracts learnings from winners and underperformers, defines exactly what to change and what to preserve in each iteration, and maintains a continuous creative velocity that prevents fatigue-driven performance collapse.

## When to Use
- Completing a creative test cycle and ready to plan the next batch based on results
- A top-performing creative is showing early fatigue signals and needs strategic iteration to extend the concept's life
- An underperforming creative had promising hook metrics but failed downstream — iteration may recover it
- Building a weekly creative planning cadence for a scaling TikTok account
- Managing a creative agency or team and need to brief specific, testable iteration directions rather than vague "let's try something new" requests

## Inputs Required
- Creative performance data for the current batch: hook rate, hold rate (25/50/75%), CTR, CPA, spend, conversion volume per creative
- Creative format, hook mechanic, and offer angle for each creative (label each)
- CPA target and current budget level
- Creative labels: which are new tests, which are scaling, which are fatiguing
- Number of new creative iterations available for the next cycle (production capacity)
- Campaign objective and conversion funnel stage being optimized

## What This Skill Does
This skill analyzes the current creative batch results, extracts the highest-signal learnings (what worked and what didn't, at each funnel stage), generates a structured iteration roadmap with specific test hypotheses, and produces a creative brief direction for each new iteration. The plan distinguishes between "extend a winner," "rescue a near-miss," and "replace a failure" as distinct iteration strategies.

## Analysis Workflow
1. Classify each creative in the current batch into one of four categories:
   - Winner: CPA at or below target with meaningful conversion volume (>5 conversions in period)
   - Near-Miss: strong hook rate and/or CTR but poor CPA — funnel failure below the click
   - Hook-Only: high hook rate but low CTR — the creative stops the scroll but fails to build desire
   - Flat Failure: low hook rate, low CTR, high CPA across all metrics
2. For Winners: identify the specific elements driving performance — hook mechanic type, creative format, offer framing, CTA language, creator style. These are "proven elements" to be preserved in all iterations from this concept.
3. For Near-Misses: identify the funnel stage where performance collapsed (click-to-conversion = landing page/offer problem, hook-to-hold = body pacing problem). Design an iteration that preserves the working hook and changes only the failing downstream element.
4. For Hook-Only failures: the hook works but the creative does not convert the initial interest. Design an iteration that tests: (a) stronger offer/benefit body, (b) social proof addition, (c) earlier CTA, (d) different offer framing in the mid-section.
5. For Flat Failures: do not iterate — replace. Design a new concept with a different hook mechanic, different format, and different offer angle. Document why this direction failed to inform future briefing.
6. Build the Iteration Roadmap for the next production cycle:
   - Column A: Winner extensions (same concept, new hook variations)
   - Column B: Near-miss rescues (same hook, new body or CTA)
   - Column C: New concept tests (fresh hook mechanic, different audience pain point, new creative format)
7. Assign production effort score to each planned iteration: Low (hook swap only, no reshoot), Medium (partial reshoot or edit), High (full new creative). Prioritize low-effort, high-signal iterations first.
8. Write a one-paragraph iteration brief for each planned creative: what proven element is being preserved, what is being changed, what hypothesis the test is designed to validate, and what a successful result looks like.
9. Forecast the testing calendar: given production capacity, when will each iteration be ready to launch, and when will each new test have sufficient data to evaluate (typically 3–5 days at target spend level)?

## Output Requirements
- Current batch classification table: Winner / Near-Miss / Hook-Only / Flat Failure per creative
- Proven elements list from Winners (hook mechanic, format, offer framing, CTA) — these are the account's confirmed creative assets
- Iteration roadmap table: iteration type (Extend / Rescue / Replace), creative name/concept, what's changing, what's preserved, production effort, launch priority
- One-paragraph iteration brief per planned creative
- Testing calendar with estimated launch dates and evaluation dates
- Production brief for creative team: how many creatives, what types, what priority order, what deadlines
- Creative velocity check: does this plan maintain 3–5 new creative introductions per week? If not, flag the gap.

## Platform-Specific Best Practices
- The highest-leverage iteration on TikTok is a hook swap on a proven creative body. It requires the creator to re-film only the first 3 seconds and can be edited cheaply — yet it creates a fully independent test with high learning value.
- Iteration cadence, not individual creative quality, is the primary predictor of sustained TikTok Ads performance. Accounts that consistently introduce 3+ new tests per week outperform accounts that rely on finding a single home-run creative.
- When iterating Winners, change only one variable at a time: hook vs. body vs. CTA vs. format. Multi-variable iterations produce ambiguous learnings and waste production budget.
- Near-miss creatives (strong hook, poor CPA) are the most valuable creative asset to iterate because the expensive creative production has already proven audience interest — the fix is in messaging or funnel, not in production.
- Document every iteration hypothesis and result, even failures. TikTok creative learning compounds over time — accounts with a documented creative intelligence library outperform accounts that start fresh each quarter.

## Guardrails
- Do not iterate a Flat Failure creative — the production cost of iteration is better spent on new concept development.
- Do not change more than one structural element per creative iteration — multi-variable tests produce unactionable learnings.
- Do not plan more iterations than your production capacity can execute — an overplanned iteration calendar that cannot be executed creates pipeline stalls.
- Never iterate in response to insufficient data — require a minimum of 3 days and 500 impressions before classifying a creative's performance and planning its iteration path.

## Example Requests
1. "Here are my results from the last 10 days across 6 TikTok creatives. Classify each one, extract the learnings, and give me a full iteration roadmap for my next production week."
2. "My best-performing creative has a hook rate of 38% but my CPA is still too high. I think the issue is in the body. Build me an iteration plan that tests 3 different body approaches while keeping the same hook."
3. "I have 4 production slots available this week for new TikTok creatives. Based on my current creative data, tell me exactly what to produce: which concepts to extend, which to rescue, and which to replace."
